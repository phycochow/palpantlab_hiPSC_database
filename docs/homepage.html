Differentiation and Maturation Protocols Influences the Functionaility of Stem Cell Derived Cardiomyocytes

We performed data mining on 300 hiPSC-CM studies (Ewoldt et al., 2024 manuscript) to find common themes that influence physiological performance and establish a clear knowledge gap in how variation in protocol parameters, like genetic background of hiPSCs, affect maturation and downstream hiPSC-CM function. It also points at the limited number and diversity within in-vitro datasets. And despite the transcriptomic insights of genome-wide CRISPR screens (Padmanabhan et al., 2024), hiPSC-CMs do not fully recapitulate complex cell and organ-level physiology that can be causal of traits (Cao et al., 2023), which shifts this project’s attention to alternative data resources. The Palpant Lab has been working to make some basic analyses of that data freely available; you can read more about the overall effort here.


Building on the initial genome-wide association analyses, we’ve now estimated the heritability of each of those traits and disorders, over 4,000 as of this writing. As with the primary GWAS analyses, we’re making the LD Score Regression SNP-heritability results available for browsing and bulk download.

If you’re interested in additional traits, additional ancestry groups, and/or other heritability estimation methods, SNP heritability estimates are also now available from the Pan-UK Biobank analysis.

We view these results as preliminary and subject to change. They rely on uniform automated GWAS of thousands of variables, each of which can potentially be refined by subject matter experts. You can view the change between our Round 1 and Round 2 results here. But we hope you’ll agree that the current results still provide an interesting and useful look at the genetics of the many diverse traits and disorders studied in the UK Biobank, and are potentially informative for highlighting phenotypes meriting further follow-up.
