Data Server for >300 Published hiPSC-CM Differentiation and Maturation Protocols


With modern advancements in cutting-edge stem cell technology, it is now possible to generate human stem cell derived cardiomyocytes (hiPSC-CMs) for the development of cardioprotective interventions. hiPSC-CMs can be used for regenerative medicine or to model ischaemic-reperfusion injury responses (oxygen deprivation) commonly found in heart diseases.

We performed data mining on 314 hiPSC-CM studies to find common themes that influence physiological performance and establish a series of protocol parameters, including the cell's genetic background, that are enriched to impact downstream hiPSC-CM function. The Palpant Group has been working to provide comprehensive analyses of that data through machine learning, epigenetics and statistical genetics. If you’re interested in additional findings, you can read more about the overall effort here.

FAQ

What is the importance of this study?

Ischaemic-reperfusion injury (oxygen deprivation) to the heart muscle remains the leading cause of death and disability worldwide. Despite decades of extensive research and successful preclinical trials, there are no clinically-approved drugs to block the acute injury response. hiPSC-CMs account for the species-specific differences in cardiac physiology, genetics, and disease progression that are overlooked in preclinical animal models. But the embryonic nature of hiPSC-CMs makes them inappropriate to model adult heart diseases. During pregnancy, embryos share oxygen with their mothers. The foetal heart operates in an environment with lower oxygen levels and is adapted to generate energy by primarily relying on oxygen-free, glycolytic metabolism, which makes cardiomyocytes resistant to ischemic-reperfusion injury. That is why disease modelling with hiPSC-CMs requires maturation protocols to recapitulate the adult state. However, these protocols are often exclusive to a few research groups and have not been systematically compared to find best protocol variables affecting hiPSC-CM function.

What does the current dataset contain?

Ewoldt et al. systematically analysed 300 studies focusing on maturation benchmarking for ventricular cardiomyocytes, which aims to improve hiPSC-CMs’ embryonic characteristics to model adult cardiac diseases (cite). In section V, the authors determined that most protocols are exclusive to research groups and not appropriately compared to other existing ones. There is insufficient data to identify protocol parameters that yield the best functional outcomes, which in turn hinders the progression of the field. They provide a dataset with 56 (study characteristics) x 300 (hiPSC studies) and urge researchers to continue adding to the starting database.

In our recent paper, we compiled evidence that glycaemic variability in diabetes is a significant risk factor for heart muscle dysfunction and ischemia . We included 14 metabolic maturation studies to Ewoldt et al.’s dataset and performed a quantitative analysis on hiPSC-CM functional outcomes to enrich for protocol parameters. We applied feature importance analysis on 8 ventricular cardiomyocyte functionality measurements
