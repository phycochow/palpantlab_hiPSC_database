Data Server for >300 Published hiPSC-CM Maturation and Differentiation Protocols

With modern advancements in cutting-edge stem cell technology, it is now possible to generate human stem cell derived cardiomyocytes (hiPSC-CMs) for the development of cardioprotective interventions. hiPSC-CMs can be used for regenerative medicine or to model ischaemic-reperfusion injury responses (oxygen deprivation) commonly found in heart diseases.

We performed data mining on 314 hiPSC-CM studies to find common themes that influence physiological performance and establish a series of protocol parameters, including the cell's genetic background, that are enriched to impact downstream hiPSC-CM function. The Palpant Group has been working to provide comprehensive analyses of that data through machine learning, epigenetics and statistical genetics. About this Server

What is the goal of this server?

Ischaemic-reperfusion injury (oxygen deprivation) to the heart muscle remains the leading cause of death and disability worldwide. Despite decades of extensive research and successful preclinical trials, there are no clinically-approved drugs to block the acute injury response. hiPSC-CMs account for the species-specific differences in cardiac physiology, genetics, and disease progression that are overlooked in preclinical animal models. But their embryonic nature is inappropriate for modelling adult heart diseases.

During pregnancy, embryos share oxygen with their mothers. The foetal heart operates in an environment with lower oxygen levels and is adapted to generate energy by primarily relying on oxygen-free, glycolytic metabolism, which makes cardiomyocytes resistant to ischemic-reperfusion injury. That is why disease modelling with hiPSC-CMs requires maturation protocols to recapitulate the adult state. However, there is a significant knowledge gap regarding how does the combination of protocols parameters affects downstream hiPSC-CM functional outcomes. These protocols are often exclusive to a handful of research groups and have not been systematically compared to find the best protocol variables that impact hiPSC-CM function. Through this server, we advocate for stronger coordination to improve our ability to benchmark and advance hiPSC-CM function.

What does the current dataset contain?

FAQ

How do I commit my experimental data?

We encourage all users to upload and QC existing data regardless of whether it is published. On this server, we offer a similarity check through the Study Database with user-defined protocol parameters to identify similar study designs and their resulting hiPSC-CM functional outcomes. This aims to help users benchmark and estimate the impact of new changes in their protocol designs.
